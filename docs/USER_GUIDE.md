# Using *Constellation* — a visitor's guide

*Constellation* (ars gratia artis) is an explorable map of art history: a zoomable
sky of movements and artists, walkable 3D galleries for each artist, a generative
soundscape, neural narration, deep‑zoom on every canvas, and a docent you can ask
anything. This guide covers everything you can do.

> The docent (the **?** button) is also taught from this guide, so you can simply
> ask it *"how do I change the language?"* or *"how does the tour work?"* and get a
> spoken or written answer.

---

## The constellation (home)

The opening view is a night sky of art **movements**; each holds the **artists**
who belong to it.

- **Zoom** — mouse wheel (or pinch on touch) zooms toward the cursor.
- **Pan** — click‑and‑drag (or one‑finger drag) moves the sky.
- **Double‑click / double‑tap** zooms in toward that point; hold **Shift** or
  **Alt** while double‑clicking to zoom back out.
- **Open a movement or artist** — click its star.
- **✦ Explore** (top‑left) opens a search panel. Switch between the **Movements**
  and **Artists** tabs, type to filter, and click a result to fly there or open it.

### The artist card

Clicking an artist opens a card with a portrait, dates, nationality, movement, and
a biography.

- **Read the full biography** — the bio scrolls inside the card; use the mouse
  wheel over the text (it scrolls the text, not the sky behind it).
- **Listen** — the *Listen* button reads the biography aloud in a neural voice.
- **Choose a museum** — pick the gallery space the works will hang in (each museum
  has its own architecture, light, and hanging style), then press **Enter** to step
  inside.

---

## Walking a gallery

Each gallery is a real 3D room you move through in first person.

### Move and look

- **Desktop** — **W / A / S / D** or the **arrow keys** walk (forward, left, back,
  right). **Click‑and‑drag** to look around.
- **Touch** — a **joystick** appears at the corner to walk; **drag** elsewhere to
  look.
- Movement keys are ignored while you're typing (e.g. a question), so the camera
  stays put.

### What's on the walls

- **Walk up to a work** and face it — the room quietly dims and the painting's own
  soundscape rises (surf, a café, wind, rain…). Step back and the room returns.
- **Click a painting** to open it: its story, facts, and links, with a **Share**
  button. Click the image (or the expand icon) to **deep‑zoom**.
- **Influence doorways** — the far wall has glowing arches that lead to the
  galleries of kindred artists who shaped, or were shaped by, this one. Walk up and
  click to travel there.
- Galleries are inhabited — drifting visitors, dust in the light, benches — and
  skylit museums drift slowly from midday toward a warm dusk over a few minutes.

## The guided tour

Press **Guided tour** (bottom centre) and a docent walks you through the room.

- It **starts from the work you're nearest to**, then moves along the walls,
  pausing before each piece while the narration plays.
- The **tour bar** shows your progress (`n / total`) and three controls:
  - the **list icon** opens **"Jump to a work"** — every work in the room; tap one
    to go straight there and continue from it,
  - **Next** skips to the following work,
  - **End tour** stops and returns you to free roam.
- **Look closer mid‑tour** — click the painting being discussed to open its
  **deep‑zoom** while the narration keeps going. In that view a **progress bar**
  shows how far through the docent's speech you are, and a **pause / play** button
  lets you freeze it and linger on a detail. Closing the zoom (or pressing Next)
  resumes and carries on; when the docent moves on, the zoom closes by itself.

---

## Deep‑zoom (studying a work up close)

Opening a painting full‑frame lets you inspect brushwork at very high resolution.

- **Zoom** — scroll; **pan** — drag; **double‑click** to zoom toward a point.
- The **bottom bar** holds everything: a **full‑screen** toggle, colour presets
  (**Original · Vivid · Warm · Crisp**), **Fit** (re‑fit the whole work), and the
  **?** docent — so the frame itself stays clear.
- **×** (top‑right) closes the view.

---

## Ask the docent (the **?** button)

A knowledgeable guide you can ask about art *or* about this experience. It appears
on the right while you roam and in the deep‑zoom's bottom bar.

- **Open it** and it greets you, then you can **speak or type**:
  - **Speak** — the microphone listens and shows a live transcript; it sends your
    question automatically after about **4 seconds of silence**, or when you press
    **Stop**. (First use asks your browser for microphone permission.)
  - **Type** — write in the box and press **Enter** or the arrow.
- **Answers** are to the point, with a small anecdote, and cite their web sources
  when the docent looked something up. A spoken question is answered aloud in the
  same neural voice; a typed one answers in **text** with a **Listen** button.
- It's **context‑aware** — it knows the museum, the artist, and the exact work
  you're standing before or zooming into, so *"tell me about this one"* works.
- **Ask about the interface too** — *"how do I walk?"*, *"how do I change the
  language?"*, *"what does the tour do?"* — and it will explain.

---

## Sound

- A layered, generative soundscape plays throughout: a cosmic pad in the
  constellation, room tone and a distant crowd in the galleries, and each work's
  own ambient scene up close. The hall's reverb matches its size.
- **Mute** — the speaker icon (bottom‑right) is the master switch. Muting silences
  **everything**, including the narration and the docent's voice; while muted the
  docent becomes a **text‑only chat** and the *Listen* buttons step aside (the text
  is always shown). Un‑mute to bring voice back.

---

## Language

Use the **EN · FR · 日本語** switcher at the top centre to change language at any
time. It re‑labels the whole interface **and** the content — artwork titles, the
stories and biographies, and the docent's spoken and written replies all follow
the language you choose.

---

## Full screen

The **full‑screen** button (bottom‑left on every view, and in the deep‑zoom's
bottom bar) expands the experience to fill your screen; press it again to exit.

## Quick reference

| You want to… | Do this |
| --- | --- |
| Zoom the sky | Wheel / pinch; double‑click to zoom in |
| Open an artist | Click their star, or use **✦ Explore** |
| Read a long bio | Scroll inside the artist card |
| Enter a gallery | Choose a museum on the card, press **Enter** |
| Walk | **W A S D** / arrows (desktop) · joystick (touch) |
| Look around | Click‑drag / one‑finger drag |
| Open a painting | Click it |
| Zoom into a canvas | Click the image, then scroll |
| Travel to a related artist | Walk through an influence doorway |
| Take the tour | **Guided tour** → it starts at the nearest work |
| Jump to a specific work in the tour | The **list** icon in the tour bar |
| Ask a question | The **?** button — speak or type |
| Hear a story or bio | The **Listen** button |
| Change language | **EN · FR · 日本語**, top centre |
| Mute everything | The speaker icon, bottom‑right |
| Full screen | The full‑screen button (bottom‑left / deep‑zoom bar) |
